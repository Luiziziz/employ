Akha Ama Café

Opening Hours  : 8:00 - 18:00 (closed Wednesday)

Akha Ama cafe opened in 2010 and is a cosy cafe down a hidden soi in the Santitham area, north of the Old City. There is an air conditioned indoor area and a leafy outdoor area, shaded by trees. The cafe works as a social enterprise, so the coffee farmers are given a fair wage and all profits go back to the village that grows the beans. Most of the staff and the founder of Akha Ama come from the hill tribe village and are very knowledgeable about coffee. The cafe as well as the founder, Lee Ayu, have become quite famous in Thailand and on weekends and public holidays the cafe is busy with visitors. Although there are a range of hot and cold drinks served at Akha Ama Café Chiang Mai, the main focus being on coffee. As well as machine made coffee (a hot americano costing 40 Baht), cold brew, coffee jelly and pour over coffee options are also available. Akha Ama cafe also has a small range of cakes including blueberry cheesecake at 65 Baht.

Doppio Ristr8to Coffee Shop 

Opening Hours : 7:08 -18:00

Website : https://www.facebook.com/ristr8to/

Doppio Ristr8to is widely considered to be the best coffee shop in Chiang Mai. The owner worked for many years in Australia, won awards for his latte art and brought back the secret skills of good coffee to Chiang Mai. The shop on Nimmanhaemin Road is very small with lots of low tables, and is always full. It has an indoor area that is air conditioned and lots of outdoor seating in the sun. Doppio Ristr8to isn’t the type of cafe to sit hours in, and people often come to drink their coffee, take photos and then leave. Doppio Ristr8to only serves coffee and one non-caffeinated chocolate drink. Their coffee menu is extensive and offers different coffee styles from around the world including the flat white, cortado, Melbourne and their famous signature drink the Doppio Ristr8to. They also offer customers the choice of origin for their beans and have a wide variety from Thai to Colombian or Ethiopian. A cappuccino made with Thai beans costs 68 Baht, and with imported beans costs 88 Baht. There are also waffle sticks and ice cream available as snacks.

Bays Café Chiang Mai Chiang Mai Riverside Café

Opening Hours  : 07:30 - 20:00 

Bays Café Chiang Mai focuses on making coffee using the pour over method so there is no machine, just an impressive display of drip coffee devices for you to watch your coffee brew. Bays Cafe is attached to a restaurant called Food4Thought and they share a large open air area in a relaxing garden. There is a roof and indoor area to sit in, or you can sit at the coffee bar with barista Bay himself. Bays Cafe has a small menu of different coffee selections - hot or iced americanos or lattes (between 65 and 85 Baht), but Bays also imports a lot of different beans from around the world and has a good choice of single origins to choose from. Ask him what beans have recently arrived as it changes weekly. The Food4Thought restaurant serves a wide variety of Thai fusion food with vegetarian, gluten free and vegan options. Their popular wrap salads start at 129 Baht and their menu also has burgers, pasta and unusual starters such as avocado fries. Open Tuesday to Saturday, last orders at Bays cafe or Food4Thought is at 20:00.

Asama Café Chiang Mai Hidden Coffee Shop in Chiang Mai

Opening Hours  : 8:00 -16:00 (closed 1st and 15th day of the month) 

Asama Cafe is a small coffee shop with a great view of Doi Suthep Mountain. It is about a 15 minute drive from the old city and hidden in a gated community. It is hard to find but worth it if you are looking for hidden gem. The cafe itself is one small room with four stools and a large, shiny, imported coffee machine. There are more seats in the outdoor area, including a two person swinging bench, which overlook a pond and the mountain. While Asama Cafe is far from the Old City of Chiang Mai it is worth the journey for coffee enthusiasts. Asama’s owner Mook is one of Thailand’s judges for the national barista championships and serves up very good coffee from a blend of imported beans. Her Café is a place for her to practice her art. A cappuccino costs 60 Baht and the menu at Asama Cafe also has some interesting coffee options including a sparkling espresso and espresso con panna both at 90 Baht.

Overstand Café Chiang Mai High Quality Coffee Shop in Chiang Mai Old City

Opening Hours : 8:00 - 14:00 (closed Tuesday) 

Overstand Café Chiang Mai offers breakfast, brunch and lunch, as well as expertly made coffee. While you might not notice the cafe from a distance, it is one of Chiang Mai’s favourite cafes for expats and tourists. The cafe is set in a converted shophouse and decorated in grey and black with lots of plants at the entrance. There are 5 small tables, a large table that customers can share and a sofa to relax on. The staff are very friendly and greet you with a smile when you arrive. Overstand Café Chiang Mai is located along a small soi close to Thapae gate in the Old City and closes at 14:00 daily. The owner is a barista from Australia so the coffee is very good. Overstand also has tea and smoothie options for those who don’t drink coffee. Their menu has regular coffee options and a special coconut espresso at 85 Baht. Overstand serves a variety of sandwiches for breakfast as well as muesli compote, and the most popular lunch option is the Cubano sandwich. The Cubano is filled with pulled pork, ham, pickles, mustard and cheddar cheese and costs 160 Baht.
